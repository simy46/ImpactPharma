- id: Q8
  category: Methodology
  question: >
    What are the objectives of the study (both the primary and the secondary objectives)?
    List them in the chronological order in which they appear.
  type: yaml list in string
  extra_specification: >
    Keep the exact phrasing from the article.
    If the article does not explicitly label objectives as primary or secondary,
    classify them based on the order in which they appear:
    the first objective(s) = Primary, the following = Secondary.
    Add the status in brackets: "[Primary]" or "[Secondary]".
    If the classification is inferred (not explicitly stated), add "[Chronological order]".

    IMPORTANT:
    Objectives describe WHAT THE AUTHORS INTENDED TO EVALUATE.
    They are intentions, not measured variables.
    They must never contain concrete endpoints, metrics, or results.
    Objectives and outcomes are fundamentally different concepts.
    Do not reuse or paraphrase any outcomes when writing objectives.

- id: Q15
  category: Outcomes
  question: Describe all the outcomes of the study (primary and secondary). Indicate their status in brackets.
  type: yaml list in string
  extra_specification: >
    Follow exactly the order and status ("[Primary]" or "[Secondary]") used in Q8.
    
    IMPORTANT:
    Outcomes describe WHAT WAS ACTUALLY MEASURED in the study.
    They must always come from the measurement sections (Methods/Results)
    or from tables containing quantitative or qualitative endpoints.

    Outcomes must:
      - be concrete measurable variables or endpoints,
      - NEVER reuse wording from objectives,
      - NEVER describe intentions or study goals.

    Under no circumstances should objectives and outcomes share the same phrasing.
    Outcomes must reflect metrics (e.g., BP reduction, adherence score, mortality rate, lab values).
